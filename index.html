Chicken Tikka Masala is a beloved dish that has won the hearts of foods enthusiasts worldwide, known for its rich, creamy, and flavorful tomato-based sauce paired with grilled chunks of chicken.Often considered as a classic example of British-Indian cuisine.Chicken tikka masala is now a true British national dish, not only because it is the most popular, but because it is a perfect illustration of the way Britain absorbs and adapts external influences.

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Tikka:

  • 500g boneless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (or paprika for less heat)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for grilling)

For the Masala Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons butter or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 can (400g) of tomato puree or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Kashmiri chili powder (or paprika for less heat)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves), for garnish

Instructions:

Marinate the Chicken:

a bowl, mix together yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric, garam masala, cumin, Kashmiri chili powder, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Add chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

Grill the Chicken:

Preheat your grill, grill pan, or oven broiler. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers. Brush with vegetable oil. Grill the chicken until it's charred and cooked through, turning occasionally. This should take about 10-15 minutes. Remove and set aside.

Prepare the Masala Sauce:

In a large pan, melt the butter or ghee over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute until fragrant. Stir in the tomato puree, chili powder, garam masala, coriander, and turmeric. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add the grilled chicken pieces to the sauce, mix well, and cook for another 5-10 minutes. Lower the heat, add the cream or coconut milk, and stir well. Season with salt and sugar (if using). Simmer for another 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.

Garnish and Serve:

Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro.

Serve hot with basmati rice or roti. Enjoy your homemade Chicken Tikka Masala! Adjust the level of spices and cream to your preference.
